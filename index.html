The Three Hansen Parameters

A comprehensive framework for understanding molecular interactions through three distinct force components.

δD

Dispersion Forces

London dispersion interactions arising from temporary dipoles in molecules.

Key Properties:
  • Universal to all substances
  • Temperature dependent
  • Weakest intermolecular force
δP

Polar Forces

Permanent dipole-dipole interactions significant in polar molecules and affect solubility.

Key Properties:
  • Orientation dependent
  • Stronger than dispersion
  • Critical for polar solvents
δH

Hydrogen Bonding

Special dipole-dipole interactions involving hydrogen atoms bonded to electronegative atoms.

Key Properties:
  • Strongest intermolecular force
  • Highly directional
  • Dominant in protic solvents

The Hansen Sphere Concept

Hansen Solubility Parameters define a three-dimensional space where solubility is predicted by the distance between a solute and solvent.

Ra² = 4(δD₁ - δD₂)² + (δP₁ - δP₂)² + (δH₁ - δH₂)²

The factor of 4 on the dispersion term accounts for its relatively larger contribution to total solubility.
